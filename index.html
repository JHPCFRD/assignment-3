On Campus Dining!

If you're ever feeling hungry after you just got done with an hour and fifteen minute class at the Petty Building, come down Tate Street. It's barely five minutes away, and it's a popular place many students go to whenever they start to feel the rumblies. From burgers to pizza to noodles, they have it all. Here are my three recommendations to satiate those cravings.

3. East Coast Wings

You can't go wrong with wings and burgers with a side of fries. East Coast Wings offers a wide variety of each, and shareables if you're feeling generous with your friends

Bone-In Wings

Variety of flavors with a variety of spice levels mixed with juicy wings.

The Big "Dill" Burger

Two seasoned quarter-pound Angus beef patties stacked with fried pickle chips, Thousand Island, American cheese, Applewood smoked bacon, tomato, and crisp lettuce on a lightly toasted bun.

ECW+G Buffalo Chicken Salad

Grilled chicken tossed in our national award-winning wing sauce (choose one of our seven heat levels) on a bed of freshly chopped romaine lettuce, diced tomatoes, sliced cucumbers, green peppers, aged shredded cheddar and mozzarella cheeses, bacon bits and seasoned tortilla crunchies.

2. Slices

Best for its quick two slice combo, you get your money's worth by choosing two of the many pizzas of your choosing. However, it's not just pizzas. There's also a multitude of pasta dishes, sandwiches, and even soups.

Baked Ziti Pizza

Tomato sauce, white sauce, Parmigiano cheese, basil.

Chicken Philly Sub

Chicken, American cheese, mushrooms, onions & green peppers, lettuce, tomatoes, mayonnaise.

Spaghetti With Meatballs

Pasta with tomato sauce, homemade meatballs, Parmigiano cheese & basil.

1. Takara Noodles

Whether it's ramen or stir-fried noodles you want, Takar Noodles has it. As a bonus, if you promote the restaurant on your social medias, you can get one of the listed sides for free.

Veggie Ramen Soy Broth

Ramen noodle in a soy broth with bean sprouts, fried tofu, scallion, and spinach.

Red Spicy Ramen

(Choice of pork, chicken or shrimp) Pork bone broth, egg, bean sprouts, scallion, seaweed, onion.

Miso Ramen

(Choice of pork, chicken or shrimp) Miso base broth, egg, bean sprouts, corn, and scallion.

BTW

Slices is a place that I'd frequent with my friend because of its affordable price. If there's any place you want to become a regular at, it's there!
While I'd say it's worth trying every place at Tate Street especially since you'll probably be near there throughout the semester, these were my recommendations to either start with or stick with! Thank you for checking out my blog!
